01
Welcome Email
The First Impression

This is the first email a new subscriber gets. The warmth in the copy was already there — I kept all of that. What I wanted was for the design to match how good the writing feels.

Design
A dark background and brand colours that give the email a strong, confident identity from the moment it opens.
Welcome moment
The subscriber's name overlaid on a bold athlete photo. It feels like an arrival into something worth being part of.
eBook cards
Each free guide got its own numbered, clickable card with a title and a short description. Much easier to see what you're getting.
Gmail tip
Two simple steps instead of a long paragraph. Anyone can follow it without having to read it twice.

02
Article Showcase
Three Guides,
One Email

This email points subscribers to three of your best articles. I wanted each one to feel like a proper feature worth clicking, so I gave each article its own space and its own moment.

Layout
Pure black background. A different feel from email one while still sitting in the same brand world.
Article cards
Full-width photo, bold title, red button at the bottom. Each article gets the space it deserves.
Photography
Each card has a photo that matches what the article is about. Body types, training, supplement bottles.
Copy
The warm opener is kept exactly as it was. I just tidied it up slightly so it flows better.

03
Weekly Newsletter
Your Weekly
Performance Edge

Patrick Willis, the heavy bag, the cable rear delt fly — three strong articles in one email. I wanted the format to carry the same energy as the topics themselves, so it reads more like a newsletter you look forward to than one you skim.

Masthead
"Your Weekly Performance Edge" in big bold type at the top. Tells the reader this is a regular thing worth opening.
Hero banner
A dark athlete photo with "Train Like A Legend" sitting over it in big red type. Sets the energy before a word is read.
Contents list
A short list upfront so the reader knows what's coming before they scroll down.
Article blocks
Photo, category label, title, copy, button. Each article gets its full moment in the email.
Logo
The Total Shape logo sits at the top of every email. Every subscriber should know who it's from the second it opens.

04
Celebrity Feature
The Captain America
Transformation

"Captain America... Avenger. Ever heard of him?" — that hook is perfect. I kept it word for word and built the whole email around it so the energy carries all the way through.

Hero image
The Captain America promo image opens the email. The subscriber knows what they're in for before they read a word.
Top Secret block
A styled classified file introduces the programme. It fits the theme and has a clear button so it's obvious it's clickable.
YouTube video
The Total Shape breakdown is embedded in the email with a short intro. Subscribers can watch right there.
Macro chart
The 40/35/25 split shown as a clean horizontal bar chart. Readable at a glance.
Celebrity cards
Ronnie Coleman, Arnold, Zac Efron at the bottom with their photos and links to the Total Shape articles.
P.S.
"Who's your superhero? Reply and let me know." Invites a response. Good for the relationship and good for deliverability.
